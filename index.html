The need

«Hello. My name is John Rim. I was born in New York City 90 years ago. Since the year 2000 reside in Estonia.

Both my parents and mother’s parents were German immigrants to the United States. We respect honest, hardworking foreigners, but we also believe the government has an obligation to citizens, to control immigration, as the American people want. Not a wall like the Berlin Wall but a firm hand — with border patrollers, police officers, and military police — plus the necessary equipment and supplies.

There are neither public nor private schools, teaching military logistics as a career for young people, not in the United States or Europe. Russia has Suvorov Academies and Nakhimov Academies, but no institutions specializing in military logistics.

So we have identified a need. An unsatisfied need for military logistics academies, to support those, who patrol the border. Associated with NATO in Europe and North America, we call them NATO Peacekeepers Logistics Academies Network: The N-PLAN»

What is N-PLAN?

The N-PLAN is a distance-learning network of academies in Europe and North America for young people career development, specializing in military logistics and promoting peace-keeping activities from 2024 to 2099.

The N-PLAN was proposed by Rainbow Foundation in 2008 by Malle Koppel, later replaced by me, John Rim, and the supervisory board, which consists of Sergei Popov, Bruno Suvi and Olga Popova. N-PLAN has committees in the vicinity of Fort Bragg and Pope Army Air Field, Joint Base Charleston and Kingsley Field.

Rainbow Foundation has been filing an annual report of activity with the Estonian market registry that assigned number 90006704 to the reports beginning in 2002. All reports are open to the public on the Internet in the Estonian language. About 50 pages of reports are available in addition to the financial data required by the Ministry of Finance.

Youth Programs

Malle Koppel recruited student volunteers to participate in a five-day exhibit in Tallinn, where about 30 specialized schools, described themselves to younger students, accompanied by their teachers. The Rainbow exhibits featured practical uses of the English language in Estonia, including the use of English by the military in contact with NATO. English is an official NATO language.

John Rim recruited students for a summer program of games and sports at a small Hiiumaa island in 2002. An experiment to learn whether students would speak English, given the opportunity. Four weekly sessions attracted about 60 students. The 2002 experiment result was clearly negative — with no career link, students remained uninterested in the English language outside the context of a school.

Our Plans

In 2020, N-PLAN focuses on a handful of leaders in 30 member-states associated with NATO — and a few more aspiring young leaders such as Anna Jagonen, who was candidate Number 157 in a recent election for public office in Estonia.

The focus is on six positions in each of the 30 NATO member-states: Minister of Education, Minister of Defense, Minister of Finance, Minister of Agriculture, Minister of Foreign Contacts and Prime Minister.

By December 31, 2020, contacts will be made with approximately 180 persons in governments. There could have also been conversations among the six persons in each of these NATO member-states.

By the end of 2021, a joint conversation will take place among three member-states such as Denmark, Norway and Estonia or Croatia, Italy and Slovenia or Canada, Iceland and USA. This next step could be labeled TROIKA: three pulling in the same direction, as three horses hitched to one sleigh. Later, by the middle of 2023, the conversation among groups of six. Next, a conversation of all with NATO headquarters and a commitment from Deutschland to pay the cost of distance-learning from 2024 to 2099, which is about 75 years or three generations.
